# What Kind of AI Enterprise Are You?

An 11-profile playbook on enterprise AI spending, ROI, and governance — built from a disclosure audit of 15 non-hyperscaler enterprises (2023–2026) and a modeled cost analysis for autonomous agent fleets.

## Contents

- **`Enterprise_AI_Playbook.pptx`** — the full 14-slide deck (title, 11 persona slides, consolidated reference table, closing slide). Fully editable in PowerPoint.
- **`slides/`** — each slide exported as a standalone PNG (200 DPI), numbered `Slide_01_of_14.png` through `Slide_14_of_14.png`, for direct use in posts or docs.
- **`Funding_the_Shift_to_Autonomous_AI_Part_2.md`** — the underlying research: a modeled 2027–2028 cost analysis for a 100-agent fleet, plus architecture patterns for cost control and governance.

## Key findings

- Only 3 of 15 audited enterprises (JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, UnitedHealth Group) disclose AI spend as a discrete line item. The rest fold it into total technology budget, capex, or R&D.
- JPMorgan's technology budget: $15.3B (2023) → $19.8B (2026 guidance) — the cleanest multi-year disclosed trend in the dataset.
- Bank of America: $13.5B total technology budget, $4B+ allocated to new initiatives including AI (per company newsroom, May 2026).
- The gap between disciplined and ungoverned agent-fleet spend runs roughly 60x in documented cases — governance, not model choice, is the biggest cost lever.
